movie: 12 Angry Men (1957)
==========================

:: Title
12 Angry Men

:: Description
12 Angry Men is a 1957 American courtroom drama film directed by Sidney Lumet, adapted from a teleplay of the same name by Reginald Rose. This courtroom drama tells the story of a jury of 12 men as they deliberate the conviction or acquittal of an 18-year old defendant on the basis of reasonable doubt, forcing the jurors to question their morals and values.

:: Date
1957-04-10

:: References
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/12_Angry_Men_(1957_film)

:: Ratings
5 <mikael>,

:: Choice Themes
guilt and evidence [the story centred on whether certain evidence was enough to support a verdict of guilty beyond reasonable doubt],
sacrifice for one's beliefs [especilly juror 9 stood up alone against immense peer pressure, for his belief in justice],

:: Major Themes
working together in spite of disagreement [the 12 men were disagreeing angrily and often ended up insulting each other and havong to swallow insults in order to progress],
capital punishment [the story kept coming back to the fact that the boy would sent to the electric chair],
patricide [a boy was alleged to have killed his father],
domestic violence [the story centred around a case of domestic abuse (the father had hit the boy) which allegedly culminated in murder],
the presumption of innocence [the argument frequently came back to the point that it was the accuser's burden to prove guilt],
the inclination to believe what one wants to believe [the jury concluded that the two witnesses had in fact false convinced themelves that they saw and heard things that they most likely never did],
the desire for justice [many people wanted passionately to convict a man they thought was a killer],

:: Minor Themes
manners [some of the men were rude to others and occasionally got called out about it],
class prejudice in society [some of the mmen displayed an irrational belief in the boy's guilt apparently supported by little but the fact that he was poor and abused],
poverty in society [the squalor of the neighborhood the boy came from was pointed out],

standing up to a bully [especially juror 3 tried to bully people into agreeing with him, but juror 9 stood up to him (and others)],


movie: Rear Window (1954)
=========================

:: Title
Rear Window

:: Description
Rear Window is a 1954 American Technicolor mystery thriller film directed by Alfred Hitchcock and written by John Michael Hayes based on Cornell Woolrich's 1942 short story "It Had to Be Murder". Originally released by Paramount Pictures, the film stars James Stewart, Grace Kelly, Wendell Corey, Thelma Ritter, and Raymond Burr. It was screened at the 1954 Venice Film Festival.

:: Date
1954-09-01

:: References
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rear_Window

movie: The Wizard of Oz (1939)
==============================

:: Title
The Wizard of Oz

:: Description
The Wizard of Oz is a 1939 American musical fantasy film produced by Metro-Goldwyn-Mayer. Widely regarded as one of the greatest films of all time, it is the most commercially successful adaptation of L. Frank Baum's 1900 children's fantasy novel The Wonderful Wizard of Oz. Directed primarily by Victor Fleming (who left the production to take over the troubled Gone with the Wind), the film stars Judy Garland as Dorothy Gale alongside Ray Bolger, Jack Haley, and Bert Lahr.

:: Date
1939-08-25

:: References
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Wizard_of_Oz_(1939_film)

:: Ratings

human self improvement [Dorothy's companions were all trying to improve themselves in one way or the other],
loyalty to one's family [the story concluded with Dorothy realizing how important her family was to her],

:: Major Themes
master and pet [Dorothy and Toto],
wicked witch [Dorothy made an enemy of the wicked witch of the West who thought Dorothy had killed her sister witch],
escape from reality [Dorothy was alluding beforehand to the magical land she later came to visit when knocked out during the storm],
friendship [Dorothy made several fast friends on her way to Emerald city],

:: Minor Themes
