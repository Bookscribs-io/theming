
novel: Never Let Me Go (2005)
=============================

:: Title
Never Let Me Go

:: Author
Kazuo Ishiguro

:: Description
The story follows Kathy and some of her friends from childhood to early adulthood. 
It is gradually revealed that they are all clones that will have to donate their organs and thus die young.

2005

novel: The Fall (1956)
======================

:: Title
The Fall

:: Author
Albert Camus

:: Description
Set in Amsterdam, The Fall consists of a series of dramatic monologues by the self-proclaimed "judge-penitent" Jean-Baptiste Clamence, as he reflects upon his life to a stranger. In what amounts to a confession, Clamence tells of his success as a wealthy Parisian defense lawyer who was highly respected by his colleagues; his crisis, and his ultimate "fall" from grace, was meant to invoke, in secular terms, The Fall of Man in the Garden of Eden. The Fall explores themes of innocence, imprisonment, non-existence, and truth. 

:: Date
1956

:: Ratings
5 <Mikael>,

:: Choice Themes
coping with mortality [Clamence talks about immortality and death a lot],
self-love [Clamence admits his egotism in just this word],
absurdist philosophy [the novel illustrates Camus' Absurdism],
nihilism [there is no objective truth in this world and God is dead],

:: Major Themes
the desire for fame [young Clamence desires fame],
righteousness [young Clamence is certainly rightous],
vanity [Clamence is patently vain],
lust [Clamence reflects on his womanizing],
womanizing [Clamence reflects on his womanizing],
contempt [Clamence feels a lot of contempt for this and that],
legal proceedings [Clamence describes various bits and pieces],
romantic love [old Clamence laments he cannot achieve],

:: Minor Themes
coping with humiliation [car incident at p34],
