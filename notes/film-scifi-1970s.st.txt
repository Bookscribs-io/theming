movie: Colossus The Forbin Project (1970)
=========================================

:: Title
Colossus: The Forbin Project

:: Description
The film is based on the 1966 science fiction novel Colossus, by Dennis
Feltham Jones (writing as D. F. Jones), about an advanced American defense
system, named Colossus, becoming sentient to everyone's pleasant surprise...
at first. After being handed full control, Colossus's draconian logic expands
on its original nuclear defense directives to assume total control of the
world and end all warfare for the good of mankind despite its creators' orders
to stop.

:: Date
1970-04-08

:: References
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Colossus:_The_Forbin_Project

:: Ratings
4 <paul>,

:: Genre
sci-fi

:: Major Themes
autonomous weapons system [The Colossus computer was designed as an advanced national defense system that functioned without human aid. The Soviets had coincidently built a similar system at the same time.],
autonomous weapons system killing everyone [The Colossus defense computer got out of human control forced everyone to put an end to all warfare for the good of humankind.],
sentient computer [The Colossus super computer became sentient, as did its Soviet counterpart.],
human vs. machine [The people of Earth versus the Colossus computer.],
maker and machine [Dr. Charles Forbin and the Colossus computer.],
nuclear holocaust [The Colossus computer come to control the nuclear weapons stockpiles of the United States and the Soviet Union.],
machine controlled society [The film ended with the Colossus computer taking over the world and forcing humans to live together in peace.],

:: Minor Themes
video phone [The scientists and politicians communicated over bulky and stationary video phones.],
government concealing information [Both United States and Soviet governments concealed knowledge that their defense computers fired missiles from their respective publics.],
romantic love [Charles Forbin and Cleo Markham.],
the need for freedom [Charles Forbin told Colossus that people needed freedom to exist, but Colossus felt that freedom was an illusion.],


movie: Solaris (1972)
=====================

:: Title
Solaris

:: Description
A meditative psychological drama occurring mostly aboard a space station orbiting the fictional planet Solaris. The scientific mission has stalled because the skeleton crew of three scientists have fallen into separate emotional crises. Psychologist Kris Kelvin travels to the Solaris space station to evaluate the situation only to encounter the same mysterious phenomena as the others.

:: Date
1972-05-13

:: References
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Solaris_(1972_film)

:: Ratings
5 <paul>,

:: Genre
sci-fi

:: Major Themes
the limits of science [Was the mysterious world ocean on Solaris too mysterious for science to explain?],
problem of language and meaning [The Solaris world ocean appeared to be trying to communicate with the humans on the space station in orbit around it.],
living planet [The Solaris world ocean appeared to be a living being of some kind.],
romantic love [Kris and the Hari replica.],

:: Minor Themes
space station [The bulk of the film took place on the space station Solaris.],
space travel [Kris flew from Earth to the Solaris station, which orbited a mysterious planet.],
coping with memories of a lost love [Kris over his suicided wife Hari.],
personal identity [To what extent was the Hari that the world ocean conjured up the same as the Hari who killed herself 10 years prior.],
what if I found myself in a zero gravity environment [Kris and Hari experienced a 30 second period of weightlessness while aboard the Solaris space station.],
mother and son [Kris encountered a replica of his mother aboard the Solaris space station.],


movie: Westworld (1973)
=======================

:: Title
Westworld

:: Description
Westworld is a 1973 American science fiction Western thriller film written and
directed by novelist Michael Crichton. Its plot concerns amusement park
androids that malfunction and begin killing visitors. It stars Yul Brynner as
an android in a futuristic Western-themed amusement park, and Richard Benjamin
and James Brolin as guests of the park.

:: Date
1973-11-21

:: References
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Westworld_(film)

:: Ratings
4 <paul>,

:: Genre
sci-fi

:: Major Themes
android [There was an amusement park full of androids that went haywire.],
what if I could do anything I wanted without repercussions [The Delos amusement park was centered around the idea that park goes could lose themselves and do anything they wanted without constraint.],
human vs. machine [There was an amusement park full of androids that went haywire and started killing everyone.],
AI safeguards [The people running the park were unjustifiably confident that safeguards were in place to keep their androids from running amok.],

:: Minor Themes
hovercar [The amusement park goes were ferried to the pack location in a big hovering vehicle.],
sex-droid [Peter Martin had full sex with a female android at the brothel.],
coping with a bad hangover [Peter Martin and John Blane woke up with bad hangovers after the bar fight.],

:: Settings
Wild West, Medieval times, Ancient Rome,
