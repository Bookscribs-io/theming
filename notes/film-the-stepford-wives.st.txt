movie: The Stepford Wives (1975)
================================

:: Title
The Stepford Wives

:: Description
A photographer and young mother begins to suspect that the frighteningly submissive housewives in her new idyllic Connecticut neighborhood may be robots created by their husbands. It is based on the 1972 Ira Levin novel of the same name..

:: Date
1975-02-12

:: References
https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Stepford_Wives_(1975_film)

:: Ratings
4 <paul>,

:: Genre
sci-fi

:: Major Themes
what if people around me started behaving strangely one by one [Joanna watched the Stepford women turn into obedient drones for their husbands and worried that the same was ultimately in store for her.],
husband and wife [The story followed the Eberhart's as the moved from New York City to the strange town Stepford with their two young children.],
sexism in society [Walter Eberhart joined an elite men only club Stepford. Joanna and her friend Bobbie tried to raise awareness among the other women in Stepford that it was sexist to have a such a club, and they tried to form a counter women only club. More generally, the men of Stepford preferred for women to be obedient housewives and went about doing in their wives and replacing them with just such replicas.],
friendship [Bobbie Markowe befriended Joanna, who was new in town and didn't know anybody.],
disintegrating romantic relationship [Joanna's relationship slowly broke down as he became more and more involved in the clandestine Stepford Men's Association.],

:: Minor Themes
dealing with children [Walter became exasperated with having to entertain seven kids at his house.],
the desire to be remembered [Joanna told a photo critic that she was into photography because she wanted to be remembered for something.],
questioning one's sanity [Joanna mused to a female psychiatrist that she might be crazy to think that the men of Stepford were somehow changing their wives into submissive automaton-like drones, but she was still convinced it was happening all the same.],
